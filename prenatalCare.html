Prenatal Care

What is Prenatal Care?

Prenatal care is the health care received during pregnancy, involving regular checkups with a healthcare provider. It is essential for monitoring the health of both the mother and the developing baby.

Importance of Prenatal Care

  • Health Monitoring: Regular checkups help monitor the health of the mother and baby.
  • Preventive Measures: Prenatal care can prevent potential health issues through early intervention.
  • Personalized Care: Tailored care based on individual health needs.

Components of Prenatal Care

  • Regular Checkups: Scheduled visits to a healthcare provider.
  • Physical Exams: Includes weight checks, blood pressure monitoring, and urine tests.
  • Prenatal Testing: Tests to check for genetic conditions and other health concerns.

Recommended Schedule for Visits

  • First Trimester: Initial visit within the first 8-10 weeks of pregnancy.
  • Subsequent Visits: Every 4 weeks until 28 weeks, then every 2 weeks until 36 weeks, and weekly until delivery.
